Step 1 of 4
Create a Spotify App

You need a free Spotify developer app to let this widget read your currently playing track. This takes about 2 minutes.

Why do I need this? Spotify requires apps to register before accessing your listening data. It's free, takes 2 minutes, and your data only goes between your browser and Spotify — never anywhere else.
  • 1
    Go to developer.spotify.com/dashboard and log in with your Spotify account.
  • 2
    Click Create app. Give it any name and description (e.g. "Stream Widget").
  • 3
    In the Redirect URIs field, paste exactly:

    https://dexteritycs.github.io/Spotify-widget/
  • 4
    Tick the Web API checkbox, then click Save.
  • 5
    Open your new app's Settings — you'll need the Client ID and Client Secret on the next step.
Open Spotify Dashboard ↗
Step 2 of 4
Connect Your Spotify

Paste in the credentials from your Spotify app, then click Connect to authorise the widget.

Where do I find these? On your Spotify app page at developer.spotify.com, click Settings. Client ID is shown directly. For Client Secret, click View client secret.
Found under Settings in your Spotify app dashboard
Keep this private — it's like a password for your app

Step 4 of 4
Add to Streamlabs OBS

Add the widget as a Browser Source in SLOBS. It'll show up on your stream and automatically display what you're listening to.

➕
In SLOBS, click Add Source in your scene and choose Browser Source.
🔗
Paste this URL:
https://dexteritycs.github.io/Spotify-widget/
📐
Set width to 340 and height to 110. Position it wherever you like on screen.
🎨
In Custom CSS, paste: body { background: transparent !important; } — this makes the background transparent.
✅
Click Done. The widget will show your currently playing Spotify track automatically.
💡 Pro tip — While the widget is on screen, open the Interact window in SLOBS and press ` (backtick) to open a quick menu where you can log out or change colours without leaving your stream.
